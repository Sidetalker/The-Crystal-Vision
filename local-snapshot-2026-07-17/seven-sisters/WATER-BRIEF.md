## 1. Lake Eyre Basin (LEB)

### Science
- One of the world’s large **endorheic** (inland-draining) basins; on the order of **~1.2 million km²**.
- Covers large parts of inland **Queensland, South Australia, Northern Territory**, and some of western **NSW**.
- Major dryland river systems include the **Georgina, Diamantina, Cooper Creek** and others that can feed toward **Kati Thanda–Lake Eyre**.
- Flow is **highly variable**: long dry periods, rare big floods (“boom and bust”).
- Lake Eyre itself is often dry or partly dry; large fills are **episodic**, not guaranteed annual events.
- Evaporation in the arid interior is extreme — even big inflows can disappear.

### Vision (CrystalCore)
- **Priority 1 water rail:** “inland pulse / new waters” as a **care and imagination** theme.
- Songline Path 1–3 language may *point* here as story — **not** as proof a fill is underway.

## 2. Great Artesian Basin (GAB)

### Science
- Among the world’s largest groundwater basins; on the order of **~1.7 million km²** under much of arid and semi-arid Australia (parts of QLD, NSW, SA, NT).
- Water sits in **porous sedimentary rock** (aquifers); pressure can create **artesian** conditions and natural **springs**.
- Recharge is slow and localized compared with the scale of the basin — **not an infinite tap**.
- Springs and soaks can be **ecologically critical** and **culturally significant** to Aboriginal communities.
- Historical waste (uncapped bores, open drains) damaged pressure and springs; reform and better practice matter.
- Public bulk “storage” numbers are huge; **sustainable usable take** is a different, harder question (science + governance).

### Vision (CrystalCore)
- **Priority 2:** deep / hidden water · Path 5 “protect what is hidden.”
- Honour springs; **never invent sacred GPS** or claim OS “filled” the basin.

### Public links
- https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Great_Artesian_Basin  
- https://soe.dcceew.gov.au/inland-water/environment/water-sources

## 3. Murray–Darling Basin (MDB)

### Science
- Australia’s major southern **food-bowl** river system; large population and agricultural dependence.
- Subject to **drought cycles**, allocation politics, ecological stress, and reform debates.
- Not the same system as Lake Eyre Basin (different drainage), though both shape the continental water story.
- “More water forever by decree” is not a scientific plan — real levers are policy, rights, climate, land use, and infrastructure.

### Vision (CrystalCore)
- **Priority 3:** **care and repair**, not extraction myth or fantasy inland flood of the MDB.
- Path 4 (right way) applies: no wrong approach to Country or people.

### Public links
- https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Murray%E2%80%93Darling_basin  
- https://www.mdba.gov.au/ (Murray–Darling Basin Authority — official)
